Reasons for Toxic Air

3. STREET CANYONS- Poor Urban Design

A 'Street Canyon' is defined as a confined urban space surrounded by buildings on both sides of a street, where natural air ventilation is limited, leading to the accumulation of pollutants from vehicle emissions and other sources, posing health risks to pedestrians and residents.

Street canyons vary in structure, including the aspect ratio (AR), asymmetry etc, which significantly influence wind field and pollutant dispersion within the street canyons.

Radiative geometry of canyon contributes to lowering the effective albedo of the urban environment, which means it absorbs more incoming solar radiation, mainly because of the multiple reflection of radiation between the canyon surfaces.

This same phenomenon is also responsible for poor signal reception in cell phones and TV signals in densely populated areas.
