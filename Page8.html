Reasons for Toxic Air

8. TOPOGRAPHICAL CONSTRAINTS- Location, Temperature and Humidity

India's geography, particularly the Indo-Gangetic Plains and Himalayan foothills, traps pollutants due to low wind speeds and basin-like terrain, limiting dispersion of PM, SO2, and ozone. This affects northern India, where flat plains and Himalayan barriers create a "pollution bowl", exacerbating winter smog with primary and secondary pollutants as air pollution becomes trapped due to a combination of factors, including topography and weather patterns

The pollution in the "bowl" is a result of various factors, including vehicle and industrial emissions, dust from the Thar desert and Himalayas, road dust, and other sources. Unsustainable agriculture practices in the arid and semi-arid region of the country and rampant clearing of forests broke the ceiling and created dust storms that damage property and crops. It is past time India too recognizes its own Dust Bowl and initiates a large-scale ecological restoration programme to halt it. Else, we will see increasingly intense dust storms, and a choked Delhi would be a permanent feature.

Also, India's humid climate (80 - 90% in coastal cities) and monsoon patterns increase aerosol formation (e.g., secondary PM, sulphate aerosols) and bioaerosol proliferation (e.g., fungal spores, bacteria). Humidity enhances pollutant deposition on surfaces, re-aerosolizing them later and exacerbating conditions
