Reasons for Toxic Air

9. IMPROPER WASTE MANAGEMENT - Waste Burning and Open Landfills

Improper disposal of hazardous waste substantially threatens air quality, leading to significant air emissions that contain harmful collutants.

Waste burning emits a wide range of toxic compounds, including dioxins, furans, heavy metals, CO2, PM and volatile organic compounds (VOCS), into the air, which, upon inhalation, lead to respiratory problems, cardiovascular diseases, neurological issues and even cancer upon prolonged exposure.

Open improperly managed landfills present another significant source of hazardous waste air emissions. When hazardous waste is disposed of in open landfills without appropriate containment measures, it can generate LEACHATE and GAS EMISSIONS.

Hazardous waste materials may interact with moisture in the landfill, forming leachate, a toxic liquid that can percolate through the landfill layers and eventually seep into the surrounding soil and groundwater. Leachate can release hazardous chemicals into the air when it evaporates.

Anaerobic decomposition of hazardous waste in landfills can also generate landfill gas, mainly methane, carbon dioxide, and other volatile compounds. If not captured and treated properly, these gases escape into the atmosphere, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution.
